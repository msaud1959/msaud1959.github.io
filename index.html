About me

Who I am, what drives me, and where I'm headed.

My story starts with a simple habit: whenever I see a slow, manual, frustrating process, I can't stop thinking about the software that could fix it.

The habit started in Dhaka, where I studied Computer Science & Engineering at BRAC University and learned what building under pressure feels like — a NASA Space Apps Challenge global finalist honourable mention and a Hult Prize semi-final before I'd finished second year. In 2023 I transferred to Federation University Australia to complete my Bachelor of IT in Ballarat, where I've kept a Distinction average with High Distinctions across my data and business analytics electives — and picked up a second NASA award, this time the People's Choice Winner at Space Apps Melbourne 2023.

My capstone project became something bigger. As Product Owner and Lead Developer, I led a six-person agile team building a chronic kidney disease self-management app — and after presenting it to industry leaders in October 2025, we kept going. In January 2026 I founded KidneyMate and took it through the FORGE Pre-Accelerator (supported by LaunchVic), refining the problem, the pitch and the business model with mentors and clinicians.

At the same time I joined the Centre for eResearch and Digital Innovation (CeRDI) as a Digital Content and Communications Officer — a research centre that turns decades of scientific data into public knowledge platforms. I work across projects like Visualising Australasia's Soils, handling GIS and spatial data, web content, archive digitisation, testing and stakeholder communication.

All of it points one direction: roles that bridge technology and people — project management and business analysis — where I can lead agile teams, translate between stakeholders and developers, and ship things that genuinely help.

Selected projects

Problem → my role → what I built → what it changed.

P-01

KidneyMate — CKD self-management app

Founder · Health-tech

The problem

People living with chronic kidney disease manage strict diets, fluid limits, medications and appointments every single day — mostly with paper notes and memory. Small mistakes have big consequences.

My role

Product Owner & Lead Developer of the six-person capstone team; now Founder & Project Manager of the startup — owning product direction, roadmap, team coordination, market validation and the pitch.

What I built

A cross-platform app where patients track symptoms and emotional well-being, manage medications, monitor diet and fluids, and receive automated reminders — built on a Supabase schema I designed, with authentication and custom trackers, delivered through agile sprints managed in Jira.

Outcomes

Presented to industry leaders and academics (Oct 2025); selected for the FORGE Pre-Accelerator supported by LaunchVic; now working toward pilot-readiness and App Store / Play Store launch — Australia first, then beyond.

Flutter · Dart · Supabase · PostgreSQL · SQL & database design · Jira · Agile/Scrum · Figma · Customer discovery

P-02

Visualising Australasia's Soils (VAS)

CeRDI · Research data

The problem

Decades of valuable soil and land research sit in scattered archives, legacy websites and inconsistent formats — effectively invisible to the farmers, researchers and agencies who need it.

My role

Digital Content and Communications Officer at CeRDI on this Soil CRC project — responsible for content migration, spatial data handling, QA and documentation, including the Victorian Resources Online (VRO) archive work.

What I contributed

Contributed to the refreshed VAS website; digitised and restructured legacy archive content; prepared and checked GIS/spatial datasets for the portal; managed web content and digital assets; tested portal features, wrote documentation, and verified content with researchers and stakeholders.

Outcomes

A stronger FAIR (findable, accessible, interoperable, reusable) soils knowledge system for Australia and New Zealand — and proof that real-world data projects live or die on quality, metadata and communication.

QGIS · Spatial data · Web CMS · Metadata · Digital asset management · QA & testing · Documentation

P-03

RetailSenseAI — retail decision-support system

Academic · Analytics

The problem

Small retailers collect plenty of sales data but have no affordable way to turn it into decisions — what to stock, when to discount, where the margin leaks are.

My role

Analyst and developer on a university team project — led requirements gathering, data modelling and the analytical design, and presented the system to assessors as a business case.

What I built

A decision-support system concept and prototype: cleaned and modelled retail sales data, designed dashboards surfacing trends and anomalies, and framed AI-assisted recommendations for inventory and pricing decisions.

Outcomes

A complete end-to-end business-analysis experience — from stakeholder requirements to working prototype to final presentation — and my strongest proof that analytics is only useful when it changes a decision.

Python · SQL · Data analytics · Dashboarding · Requirements analysis · Presentation
