About me

Who I am, what drives me, and where I'm headed.

My story starts with a simple habit: whenever I see a slow, manual, frustrating process, I can't stop thinking about the software that could fix it.

That habit brought me from my home country to Federation University Australia, where I'm completing my IT degree, and into the Centre for eResearch and Digital Innovation (CeRDI) — a research centre that turns decades of scientific data into public knowledge platforms. There I work as a Digital Content and Communications Officer across projects like Visualising Australasia's Soils, handling everything from GIS and spatial data to web content, archive digitisation, testing and stakeholder communication.

Alongside that, I founded KidneyMate — a chronic kidney disease self-management app — and took it through the FORGE Pre-Accelerator, pitching health-tech innovation to mentors and judges. It taught me that I love the whole journey of a product: research, requirements, design, build, and the pitch.

That's the direction I'm growing in: roles that bridge technology and people — project management and business analysis — where I can lead agile teams, translate between stakeholders and developers, and ship things that genuinely help.

03

Selected projects

Problem → my role → what I built → what it changed.

P-01

KidneyMate — CKD self-management app

Founder · Health-tech

The problem

People living with chronic kidney disease manage strict diets, fluid limits, medications and appointments every single day — mostly with paper notes and memory. Small mistakes have big consequences.

My role

Founder and developer — owned everything from customer discovery and clinical research review to UX design, database architecture, app development and the investor pitch.

What I built

A cross-platform app for diet & fluid tracking, symptom and observation logging, medication reminders, and plain-language education — designed so a patient can show their week to a clinician in one screen.

Outcomes

Accepted into the FORGE Pre-Accelerator; pitched to mentors and judges; built a working product and a validated problem statement that now drives ongoing development.

Flutter · Dart · Supabase · PostgreSQL · SQL & database design · Figma · Customer discovery

P-02

Visualising Australasia's Soils (VAS)

CeRDI · Research data

The problem

Decades of valuable soil and land research sit in scattered archives, legacy websites and inconsistent formats — effectively invisible to the farmers, researchers and agencies who need it.

My role

Digital content and communications officer on the project team — responsible for content migration, spatial data handling, QA and documentation, including the Victorian Resources Online (VRO) archive work.

What I contributed

Digitised and restructured legacy archive content; prepared and checked GIS/spatial datasets for the portal; managed web content and digital assets; tested portal features and wrote documentation; communicated with researchers and stakeholders to verify content.

Outcomes

More historical soil knowledge is now searchable, mapped and citable through a public portal — and I learned how real-world data projects live or die on quality, metadata and communication.

QGIS · Spatial data · Web CMS · Metadata · Digital asset management · QA & testing · Documentation

P-03

RetailSenseAI — retail decision-support system

Academic · Analytics

The problem

Small retailers collect plenty of sales data but have no affordable way to turn it into decisions — what to stock, when to discount, where the margin leaks are.

My role

Analyst and developer on a university team project — led requirements gathering, data modelling and the analytical design, and presented the system to assessors as a business case.

What I built

A decision-support system concept and prototype: cleaned and modelled retail sales data, designed dashboards surfacing trends and anomalies, and framed AI-assisted recommendations for inventory and pricing decisions.

Outcomes

A complete end-to-end business-analysis experience — from stakeholder requirements to working prototype to final presentation — and my strongest proof that analytics is only useful when it changes a decision.

Python · SQL · Data analytics · Dashboarding · Requirements analysis · Presentation
